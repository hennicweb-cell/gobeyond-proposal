Understanding Go Beyond – mission, context and the opportunity

Go Beyond is a children’s charity with a beautifully clear mission: giving disadvantaged children and young people aged 8 to 13 a free, brilliant week away at residential centres in Cornwall and Derbyshire. Over 30 years, more than 20,000 children have had that experience. The goal now is to welcome more than 1,000 children every year – which means growing, not just sustaining, the income that makes it possible.

Every break is fully funded by voluntary income. Go Beyond raises approximately £1.8 million per year with no government funding – an income model that reflects strong donor relationships but also a concentration risk the board takes seriously. The trusts and foundations team, now four strong and performing well, provides a stable core. The strategic priority is to build an equally structured supporter engagement function around individual giving, regular giving, legacies, challenge events, and major donors.

This is an important appointment. The Supporter Engagement and Fundraising Manager will sit at the heart of Go Beyond’s growth ambition – responsible for the campaigns, communications, stewardship and data insight that turn donor interest into long-term income. Getting it right will directly determine how many children Go Beyond can support in the years ahead.

Why a different approach is needed

Go Beyond has invested time and effort in filling this role through a standard advertised process. That process has not yet delivered the right appointment, and I do not believe that reflects badly on the organisation or the opportunity. It reflects the nature of the role itself.

The Supporter Engagement and Fundraising Manager is a genuinely demanding brief. It spans direct mail, digital campaigns, database management, storytelling, events stewardship, line management and income reporting. It requires someone who can hold all of those plates simultaneously – managing specialists, briefing freelancers, making data-driven decisions, and never losing sight of the fundraising return. That combination of skills is not commonly found among candidates who are actively looking for a new role.

The best person for this role is almost certainly already in post somewhere, doing this kind of work well, and not browsing job boards. Reaching them requires a proactive, research-led search – not an advert and a wait.

What this appointment actually requires

Having reviewed the role in detail, I want to offer a considered view of the right candidate profile – because I believe it is somewhat broader than a traditional charity fundraising background might suggest.

This is, at its core, a direct marketing role with a fundraising outcome. The right candidate does not necessarily need to have come from a charity – but they must understand that everything they do is measured against income return. The most useful frame is someone with a campaign management or client services background in a direct marketing or fundraising agency: comfortable managing multiple workstreams and specialists, ROI-focused by instinct, and experienced in keeping complex projects moving without personally executing every element.

Go Beyond needs someone who is neither purely a marketer nor purely a fundraiser, but genuinely comfortable with both – and who will not treat income targets as someone else’s concern. In practice, the right person will:

  • Project-manage across direct mail, digital, database, PR, events stewardship and annual reporting – knowing when to brief a freelancer, when to direct the digital officer or database coordinator, and when to roll up their sleeves
  • Be genuinely comfortable with data – not just running reports, but reading what the data is saying and using it to shape campaign decisions. Raiser’s Edge familiarity is desirable; curiosity about systems is essential
  • Think in terms of supporter journeys and retention – the regular giving programme currently brings in around £90,000 with significant headroom for growth through better stewardship and uplift activity
  • Translate ideas into operational delivery – creative thinking is important, but the ability to turn a campaign concept into a working plan and see it through is what this role demands
  • Collaborate easily across a small, close-knit team – campaign ideas will feed into corporate fundraising, trusts and major donors. This person cannot work in a silo

On geography: the role is fully remote, but there is a strong case for prioritising candidates in the South West and East Midlands – regions that are rich in direct marketing and charity fundraising talent. Bristol in particular has a strong cluster of charity-sector direct marketing agencies, and candidates from that world bring exactly the mix of skills this role requires.

A note on role title and candidate attraction

The way a role is titled and framed significantly affects who applies and who can be reached through proactive search. A candidate with a direct marketing or campaign management background is unlikely to be searching for “supporter engagement” – but will respond to a title that reflects the delivery-focused, income-driven nature of the work.

As part of the briefing process, I would recommend we discuss the final job title to ensure it is attracting the right search behaviour. Options such as Fundraising and Marketing Manager or Direct Marketing Manager (Fundraising) may surface stronger candidates, while keeping the fundraising imperative front and centre.

The role itself is genuinely compelling: hands-on, varied, high-impact, and with real room to grow as Go Beyond expands. That is the story we need to tell clearly to the right people.

Recommended approach and process

I recommend a bespoke retained search, combining proactive headhunting with a targeted advertised campaign. The process is designed to give Go Beyond confidence that the shortlist has been drawn from the widest and most appropriate talent pool – not just the candidates who happened to apply.

Full briefing

The search begins with an in-depth briefing call going well beyond the job description. We agree the functional and behavioural competencies, the cultural fit indicators, the geographic search focus, and how the role and Go Beyond should be positioned to attract candidates who are not actively looking. We also agree the competency questions I will use to assess every candidate I progress.

Talent mapping and proactive outreach

I build a talent pool from the ground up – mapping direct marketers, campaign managers and fundraisers across the sector. Given the profile discussed, I will focus particularly on people who have worked within or alongside charity direct marketing agencies, as well as fundraising generalists with strong campaign delivery track records. I post a targeted advert, but the proactive outreach is where the strongest candidates are found.

Weekly transparency

We meet for approximately 30 minutes each week. Go Beyond can see the talent pool as it develops – who has been identified, approached and progressed, and who has been ruled out and why. There are no black boxes. This also allows us to retune the search quickly if something is not landing as expected.

Candidate assessment

Every candidate I progress to longlist is competency interviewed against the agreed framework. Go Beyond receives a written report on each: strengths, risks and development considerations. Particular attention will be paid to assessing how candidates bridge the gap between ideas and delivery – the ability to turn a strong concept into a working operational plan is central to success in this role.

Shortlist and final interview

Go Beyond receives a shortlist of typically three to five candidates, already assessed and fully briefed on the opportunity. By the time interviews take place, the heavy lifting is done and the decision can focus on fit, chemistry and ambition.

A note on major donor development

Major donor development has been identified as a separate priority for Go Beyond. The charity has significant donors within its existing database, and the potential to grow that income line systematically with the right resource in place.

Rather than a permanent hire, a freelance or contract arrangement may be the right starting point – an experienced major donor practitioner who can come in for three to six months, build the pipeline and methodology, and leave Go Beyond equipped to run with it.

I have access to people who work in exactly this way. I would be happy to discuss this as a parallel workstream once the Supporter Engagement and Fundraising Manager appointment is underway.
